Overview

Kimberly Bryant (born January 14, 1967) is an American electrical engineer who worked in the biotechnology field at Genentech, Novartis Vaccines, Diagnostics, and Merck. In 2011, Bryant founded Black Girls Code, a nonprofit organization that focuses on providing technology and computer programming education to African-American girls. After founding Black Girls Code, Bryant was listed as one of the "25 Most Influential African-Americans In Technology" by Business Insider.

Early Life,Skills & Education

She was born and raised in Memphis, Tennessee, by her mother. She took an interest and excelled in mathematics and science in school and was self-described as a “nerdy girl”. She received a scholarship at Vanderbilt University where she studied for her Bachelors in Electrical and Electronics Engineering. But while she was studying for this degree where she became interested in computer science and decided to switch majors to Computer Science.

Career & Achievements

After graduating she focused on her career in electrical engineering industry where she worked for DuPont and Phillip Moris. Later she moved to the biotechnology and pharmaceutical companies, where she worked for Pfizer, Merck, Genentech and Novartis. She started and founded Black Girls CODE when her daughter an interested in learning computer programming. She won many awards and was named one of the 25 most influential African Americans in technology
